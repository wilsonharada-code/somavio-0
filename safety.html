Wildlife space
Source: Parks Canada / Alberta Parks

Bear & Wildlife Activity

Bear, cougar, elk and wildlife movement can affect trail access and visitor behaviour. Treat every sighting or warning as current until official sources clear it.

Active awareness: wildlife activity can change by hour, season and food availability.
Snow terrain
Source: Avalanche Canada

Avalanches

Backcountry snow conditions can shift quickly with temperature, wind, snowfall and sun exposure. Even scenic winter routes may require avalanche awareness.

Seasonal awareness: winter and spring routes require forecast checks before departure.
Fire bans
Source: Alberta Wildfire

Wildfires & Fire Bans

Smoke, fire bans, closures and evacuation notices can change travel decisions across Alberta mountain regions.

Regional awareness: check fire status and bans before campfires, backcountry trips or highway travel.
Storm shifts
Source: Environment and Climate Change Canada

Severe Weather

Mountain weather can move from calm to unsafe quickly, especially around high winds, thunderstorms, freezing rain and sudden temperature drops.

Daily awareness: always compare the town forecast with the elevation and exposure of your route.
Closures + water flow
Source: Parks Canada / Alberta Parks / Town of Canmore / Alberta River Basins

Closures & Water Flow

Closures, rockfall hazards, high streamflow and debris movement can change safe access quickly. A route can look beautiful and still be unsafe because the hazard is overhead, upstream or beneath a riverbank.

A calmer visitor is a safer visitor.

The goal is not to make the mountains feel dangerous. The goal is to make the right decision feel easy before the day begins.

Before you go

The quick mountain-readiness check

Check official conditions before leaving — not only the night before.
Respect trail closures and posted signs even when other visitors continue ahead.
Tell someone your route and expected return time.
Check mountain weather and road conditions before longer drives.
Download offline maps before heading into areas with unreliable signal.
Keep children, pets and inexperienced visitors away from fast water and unstable banks.
In any emergency: call 911 immediately. For park emergencies, follow posted instructions and contact official dispatch when directed.

Local guidance & support

Avalanche risk is invisible from the parking lot

A blue-sky day can still hide unstable snow layers. Wind loading, warming, fresh snow and terrain angle can turn a scenic plan into a serious backcountry decision.

Local learning, not shortcuts

Preventive behaviour: read the forecast, understand whether your route enters avalanche terrain and choose lower-risk alternatives when conditions are uncertain.

Local guidance & support

Wildfire awareness protects visitors and the valley

Smoke, dry vegetation, campfire restrictions and fast-changing fire behaviour can affect health, visibility, road access and local emergency capacity.

Local learning, not shortcuts

Preventive behaviour: check fire bans, avoid sparks, respect closures, monitor smoke sensitivity and keep evacuation information visible when conditions escalate.

Local guidance & support

Severe weather moves differently in the mountains

Storm cells, wind, hail, cold rain and sudden temperature drops can arrive faster at elevation than visitors expect from the town forecast.

Local learning, not shortcuts

Preventive behaviour: check official alerts, compare weather with the elevation of your route, carry layers and choose indoor or lower-exposure options when warnings increase.

Local guidance & support

Closures, rivers and debris flow belong together

Grassi Lakes rockfall, high Bow River flow, cold fast water and debris movement show the same visitor problem: the visible path may not reveal the real hazard.

Local learning, not shortcuts

Preventive behaviour: respect closure signs, stay away from unstable banks, keep children and pets far from fast water and use official alternatives until conditions are cleared.
